# Paper Check — arXiv:2604.23985

**Paper:** Representational Curvature Modulates Behavioral Uncertainty in Large
Language Models  
**Date checked:** 2026-05-08  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.23985

## Relevance

Highly relevant. The paper directly studies the curvature idea: token-level
residual-stream trajectory curvature as a predictor and modulator of next-token
entropy.

## What The Paper Actually Tests

- Models: GPT-2 XL and Pythia-2.8B for the main analyses.
- Datasets: LAMBADA long-context passages and Universal Dependencies
  short-context sentences.
- Representation: residual stream after transformer blocks.
- Curvature: raw arccos angle between adjacent token-to-token displacement
  vectors, then averaged over a short backward-looking contextual window.
- Behavioral target: next-token entropy.
- Analysis: cross-validated OLS/Pearson prediction of entropy from contextual
  curvature.
- Perturbations: additive residual-stream perturbations at intermediate layers,
  scaled to local step size, comparing full-space/random/activation-subspace
  controls against trajectory- and planar-subspace perturbations.

## Key Difference From Our Spike

Our Phase 0/extension was not a replication of the paper.

Differences:

- We used TinyStories-33M and GPT-2-medium, not GPT-2 XL or Pythia-2.8B.
- We used a small 20-prompt TinyStories-style suite, not LAMBADA/UD.
- We tested null-calibrated curvature quantiles against a shuffled step-pair
  baseline, not raw/contextual averaged curvature in CV regression.
- We used Spearman/permutation tests, not cross-validated OLS/Pearson.
- Our GPT-2-medium sweep included representative layers, but not the exact
  GPT-2 XL middle-layer regime where the paper reports peak curvature-entropy
  coupling.

The correct local conclusion is therefore not "curvature is false." It is:

> Curvature was not detected in our TinyStories-33M / GPT-2-medium short-prompt
> spike under null-calibrated curvature-quantile Spearman/permutation tests.

That is a narrower claim than the paper's claim.

Layer choice alone probably does not explain the gap: our GPT-2-medium sweep
included layer 11 of 24, the closest middle-layer analogue to the paper's
minimum-curvature regime, and curvature was still null there. A fair replication
would need the full combination of model scale, dataset, curvature definition,
and statistical protocol.

Effect-size context also matters. The paper reports modest peak correlations
around `r ~= 0.15`, relying on consistency across models, datasets, and layers
plus comparisons against control measures. Our small-n permutation gate was a
stricter falsification spike, not the same evidence standard.

## Interpretation

The paper makes the original curvature hypothesis credible at larger model
scale and on richer datasets. It also validates the general idea that
trajectory-aligned perturbations can affect entropy.

But it does not invalidate our local result:

- In our current project artifacts, curvature was null for TinyStories-33M and
  GPT-2-medium on the selected prompt suite.
- Speed/stall dynamics were supported across TinyStories-33M, GPT-2-medium, and
  the poisoned-trigger comparison.
- Phase 0.5 established a Memory Gravity diagnostic using speed stall plus
  entropy collapse around `[XYZZY]`.

## Decision

Keep the current project pivot for the near-term tool:

- Phase 3 viewer foregrounds speed/stall dynamics and entropy.
- `curvatures_q` remains a secondary diagnostic, not the headline metric.
- Phase 4 tests tangent/speed perturbations first.

Add a separate future replication track if needed:

- Use Pythia-2.8B first if training-checkpoint dynamics matter, or GPT-2 XL for
  the closest GPT-2 family comparison.
- Use LAMBADA test split or filtered Universal Dependencies sentences.
- Implement contextual averaged raw curvature exactly as the paper defines it:
  `C_k = mean(c_{k-4}, c_{k-3}, c_{k-2})`, where
  `c_i = arccos(v_i dot v_{i+1} / (|v_i||v_{i+1}|))`.
- Filter positions with sufficient context, matching the paper's `n > 6` rule.
- Scan all layers and identify the minimum-curvature / maximum-predictivity
  layer rather than checking only a few representative layers.
- Compare curvature, contextual distance/speed, and activation magnitude under
  the paper's 10-fold cross-validated OLS/Pearson protocol with Fisher-z
  averaging.
- Only promote curvature back to headline status if that replication produces a
  robust effect in our local tooling.
